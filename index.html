ON THE GROUND

Core Action Pillars.

While our newsletter scales globally, we actively implement these key NGO pillars on the ground at scale.

01

Education & Digital Literacy

Access to education is the first step toward freedom. We offer basic literacy programs and support for schooling, ensuring women can confidently navigate the digital economy.

02

Financial Independence

True empowerment requires economic self-reliance. By connecting artisans directly to global buyers, we bypass traditional middlemen and ensure 100% of the revenue stays in the village.

03

Community Leadership

We foster solidarity networks among the women. By elevating local leaders, we create a sustainable ecosystem where women mentor and uplift each other within the rural clusters.

QUESTIONS

Everything you need to know.

HerRise is a digital publication and newsletter dedicated to sharing the untold stories of rural Indian women artisans. But we go beyond journalism — alongside every story, we provide a direct marketplace for readers to purchase the crafts featured in the article, bypassing traditional middlemen entirely.
When you read an article in our newsletter, you'll see a link to buy the artisan's products directly. This connects you straight to their WhatsApp or digital portfolio. 100% of the sale goes directly to the woman. HerRise takes zero platform fees.
We partner with college student journalists and local writers who visit rural clusters. They map the micro-economies, document the art forms, and interview the women to ensure every narrative is authentic and rooted in truth.
Yes! Our daily/weekly newsletter is completely free to read. We believe in economic solidarity, not charity. By subscribing and sharing their stories, you amplify their reach to conscious consumers. And if you choose to buy, you're directly supporting their craft.
We are a publication first. We focus on the woman and her story before the product. Traditional platforms act as middlemen, taking 15-40% commissions. We just provide the journalistic storytelling and the direct marketplace infrastructure — then step back so she keeps 100% of the value.
